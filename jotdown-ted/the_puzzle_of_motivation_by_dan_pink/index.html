DAN ARIELY

As long as the task involved only mechanical skill, bonuses worked as they would be expected, the higher the pay, the better the performance.

But once the task called for rudimentary cognitive skill, a larger reward "let to poorer performance"

In eight of the nine tasks we examined across 3 experiments, higher incentives led to worse performance

Dr. Bernd Irlenbusch, LSE

We find that financial incentives .. can result in a negative impact on overall performance
